## Description:
[[Shar|The Lady of Loss]] was the goddess of Darkness & Night, not only as the absence of light but also as the metaphorical darkness that dwelled in one's heart, mind, and soul. She was also the malevolent twin sister of [[Selûne]] (the former goddess of the Moon & Life).

The domain of the [[Shar|Mistress of the Night]] was not only that of dark places, but of deeds done under darkness, of secrets, loss, hidden pain, and wanting to forget.

She was also the creator of the [[Shadow Weave]], a dark counterpart and attack upon the [[The Weave|Weave]]

> "*I am nothing. I am the empty room. The dreamless sleep. The shadow's shadow. There was no pain before my sister set the sun aflame. Now you exist to suffer until you find your way back to my embrace.*"
> — [[Shar]]

### Appearance
In religious art-work, the [[Shar|Dark Lady]] was depicted as a darkly beautiful human woman with long raven-black hair and clad all in black, swirling attire. Her eyes, large haunting spheres of purple and black; reflecting the void at the dawn of time, and when she smiled it was cold as night. When visiting mortals in dreams, her dark hair would move and swirl regardless of gravity or outside forces.
#### Avatar(s)
[[Shar]] was known appear in person, taking the form of The [[Shar|Nightsinger]]. A 12ft figure shrouded in darkness, seeping into the shadows around her. As shadows swirl across her semi-humanoid form, concealing her appearance, only allowing her purple eyes and seducing red lips to be seen. Her voice a hauntingly beautiful song, with every word lingering like sorrow and loss. It was both heart-breaking, and enchanting.

[[Shar|The Shadowy Seductress]] was most known to take the formless structure of writhing shadowy tendrils in her manifestations. These tendrils might be used to deliver a message directly into the minds of the faithful with only a touch, or they could be used to point out something important or a direction to take. A touch from one of these tendrils could also cause numbness and the capacity to tolerate pain; rather than heal, it drove the followers of Shar to push on until they died of their injuries or exhaustion.

## Personality
A force of destruction and darkness, the very existence of [[Shar]] was paradoxical; [[Shar|The Nightbringer]] was brought into existence during the creation of the [[Realms]], at the start of time. She was the living embodiment of the void, the perfect nothing that existed before she was born. She longed to return to the ancient calm of nonexistence. And so she would disrupt order, bring down established systems, and undo everything to such ends.

Her tools were temptation, subterfuge, trickery, clandestine deals, schemes, and manipulations and, to her, the means were always justified by the ends.

By her very nature, Shar was defined by loss, the foolishness of hope, and life's inherent emptiness. She was a goddess of pains and wrongs kept hidden yet never forgotten, of concealed hatred and unnatural desires that lurked in one's heart, of nursed grudges and hungered-for revenge cultivated in secret for any slight or offense, however old and cold it was.

## Worshipers
> "*Mother of Night, darken my step as I walk among the light. Hear my prayer.*"
> — A basic prayer to [[Shar]].

Members of the [[Church of Shar]] knew each other as [[Dark Followers]], or Martyrs, but were often referred to by others as Sharrans, or Darshars (a bastardization of Dark Sharran).

People turned to Shar's dark embrace in order to forget, whether it was pain at the loss of a loved one or bitterness at a betrayal. Those who blind, would also pray to her for guidance. The mentally ill, the mad, the depressed, the anarchists, and the nihilists, might also turn to her. Though they all knew her to be dark and vindictive, they came to her anyway in the desperate belief that she and her clergy would give solace.

Good priests, especially those of [[Selûne]], [[Yondalla]], and [[Ehlonna]], warned them away, but often in vain. In this way, the [[Church of Shar]] grew. Ultimately, they were simply consumed by bitterness, loss, and vengefulness.

In most cities where worshiping Shar was barred, these churches and temples were hidden because of their illegal nature. As such, worship and practice of [[Shar]] was inherently secretive. (*Worshipping [[Shar]] is illegal in all cities within [[index|The Forgotten Tomb]], even the [[The Lost City - City of Thieves|City of Thieves]]*)

Amongst her truest believes were the [[Shadow Adept]]. A secretive sect of Sharrans who were gifted the ability to wield the [[Shadow Weave]], as if there were in [[Shadowfell]].

### Tenets
[[Shar]]'s faithful were taught to transcend fear and loss suffered by members of the weaker faiths. Their most valuable commodity was their secrecy. Information was closely guarded and only revealed when it was absolutely essential. Each member of the church maintained their "Own Secret" with Shar, pertaining to their proof of devotion to her service.

Shar's followers were directed to "quench the light of the moon" by stealing gifts meant for Selûne and defeating her faithful at every opportunity. They were to never hide from the light and always take action while under the protection of darkness. According to some sects of the faith, she desired that they destroy all the deities of the universe, and undo existence itself.

The doctrine of the Sharran church placed no restrictions on crimes committed for personal gain, including but not limited to: theft, smuggling, kidnapping, murder, or underhanded business practices. Quote often though, most of Shar's followers served not for personal enrichment, but promise of her "Dark Reward": immortality by means of transition into undeath.

Clergy members followed Shar's 'Thirteen Truths', foundational beliefs that formed the core of their faith. Among these were:
- Love is a lie. Only hate endures.
- Light is blinding. Only in darkness do we see clearly.

Hidden within a holy Sharran tome was Shar's Fourteenth Truth:
- All is meaningless. And nothing endures.

## Abilities
The [[Shar|Mistress of Pain]] had the ability to send messengers or guardians or to enact her will. She would send darktentacles or undead beholders known as 'Doom Tyrants', or mysterious monsters of shadow.

Most notable among her array of powers was the ability to perfectly perceive every thing, every person, and every deed of betrayal or nihilism that was hidden or done in the dark. She could also instantly create magical [[Artefacts]] that concealed or obscured what was true or that conjured magical darkness (abilities similar to [[Mask]]).

## Relationships
The [[Shar|Mother of Night]]'s eternal enemy (until her death), was her twin sister [[Selûne]]. Their eternal war had been waged since before all other [[Entities/Gods/Gods|Gods]] existed. Their entire purposes always at ends with each other. They never forgave, nor forgot.

The first goddess of magic bore neither of her progenitors hatred, yet [[Shar]]'s very creation of the [[Shadow Weave]] would later make her an enemy of future magic wielders. The use of [[Well of Damnation]]s would further mark the [[Shar|Lady of Sorrows]] as an enemy of the [[The Weave|Weave]] due to their [[Dead Zones]], where only [[Shadow Weave]] magic would work.

[[Shar]] harboured a cold fury against [[Mask]], God of Shadows & Thievery, out of jealously of him holding what she thought ought to be hers.

For allies, she counted on [[Myrkul]], God of Death. Though before [[Myrkul]], the former god of death, [[Jergal]] admired the [[Shar|Mistress of the Night]] for her seductive grace, and unspeakable evil. [[Jergal]] even formally courted [[Shar]] for a time, knowing full well she only planned to manipulate him.

## Description:
The [[Raven Queen]] is the interloper goddess of Death, Fate, and commonly known as a god that brought Life to her faithful Shadar-kai. She is a mysterious entity believed to exist within [[Shadowfell]], and vehemently revered by the Shadar-kai.

> "*Memory is the only immortality worth endeavouring.*"
> 
> — [[Raven Queen]]

---
### Appearance
The [[Raven Queen]] did not possess or manifest a physical form, instead appearing as a shifting amalgamation of symbols, memories, and perceptions.

---
## Personality
Cold and distant, but never cruel. This nature predates even her ascension. She accepts over resistance. She is something between good & evil; the distant emotionlessness of inevitability itself.

The [[Raven Queen]] is obsessed with collecting and observing fragments of memories and essences or souls. Many sages speculate that she is in fact insane, believing her methods and behaviour little more than aimless or desperate attempts to prevent her fragile existence from fading into oblivion. Others maintain that there is a cosmological function that is performed by the Queen's actions, as she purified souls by forcing mortals to deal with their fears and pains.

Regardless of belief, the [[Raven Queen]] has concealed her true intentions (purposely, or not) from all known beings—perhaps as a survival mechanism born from her ascension.

## Worshipers
> "*........*” (Silence)
> 
> — A common prayer to the [[Raven Queen]]

Many turn to the [[Raven Queen]]; Shadar-kai, non-evil Grave Clerics, assassins (believing their actions to be fate), and any who seek meaning out of death, be it for a lost loved one, or one's final moments. Some fearing undeath follow the [[Raven Queen]], believing in her to save their soul.

Worship for the [[Raven Queen]], is often quiet, reserved, unassuming, offerings of memories, knowledge, names, or tokens of sentimentality. Often taking place at graveyards, quiet locations (such as a private bedroom), personal shrines, sites of death (battlegrounds).

Almost no records exist of the [[Raven Queen]]'s communications with her followers. She exhibits her will across memories, emotions, dreams, and often silence. Sometimes in silence do we accept.
#### Tenets
- Death is sacred, undeath is an abomination
- Preserve memory, for it is the truest form of immortality
- Accept finality, in all its forms
- Fate cannot be escaped, only fulfilled

## Abilities
The [[Raven Queen]] is said to collect, alter, and preserve memories of mortal beings. She is also credited with the ability to guide souls on their path from death into the afterlife, steering them away from avenues of manipulation or undeath. Due to her inherent connection and dominion over death, it was believed she could even stop corrupting magic over dead beings and their souls.

Almost ironically, many believe she possesses an unobstructed perception of fate itself—seeing not possibility, but inevitability.

## Relationships
The [[Raven Queen]] has had many interesting relationships over prolonged life.

She was first a beloved queen by people all throughout the [[Feywild]] and into the [[Material Plane]]. Many other mortals such as [[Titania, Queen of the Fairies]], and [[Oberon, King of the Fairies]] were fiercely loyal and respecting of her.

Allied deities were few and far between. [[Mask]] had a fascination and respect for the [[Raven Queen]] due to her being able to keep her true intentions, name, and appearance a complete mystery to all. Likewise, [[Oghma]] coveted the [[Raven Queen]]'s collection of memories and her wealth of knowledge. [[Selûne]] was a fierce ally of the [[Raven Queen]] for many reasons, even before her ascendency to divinity, though this later waned due to the [[Raven Queen]]'s accidental corruption of Fey beings.

For enemies, the [[Raven Queen]] had many. [[Atropal]] as the god of undeath, fights a never ending battle to protect souls from [[Atropal]]'s grasps. Further, the [[Raven Queen]] has a strong dislike for [[Myrkul]] due to his ascendency against death itself, manipulating fate for his own selfish purposes. It is also believed by some, that the [[Raven Queen]] wishes to replace all older death gods, and become the true arbiter of death. On the same theme of undeath, the [[Raven Queen]] hated all those that sought - and succeeded - to cheat death, liches, and [[Shar]]'s promises to her followers alike.

Lastly, the [[Raven Queen]] fights a losing battle against patrons holding their warlock's soul as payment: often fiendish beings.

## History
Although a complete history, origin, and motivations of the [[Raven Queen]] are unknown, it is generally believed that she was once the beloved [[Raven Queen|Queen of Elves]] from the [[Feywild]]. Upon her first-hand witnessing the horrors to come during [[History of the Forgotten War|The Great Forgotten War]], and worrying that it would rip the [[Feywild]] and its elven population asunder, she rallied her followers to perform an incredibly power ritual in order to become a deity, in hopes she could appeal to greater powers and end the war before it unravelled into the hell-scape she foresaw.

The ritual required offerings of her followers souls and their magic to consolidate into her own, enabling her to reach beyond. Even before the ritual, [[Raven Queen|The First Elven Queen]] possessed power that rivalled that of lesser deities of the time, such as [[Yondalla]], who had not yet ascended to her current prominence. Her most faithful and loyal followers, the Shadar-kai believed her capable of unifying the great species of the Fey under one banner to preserve all of [[Elvenkind]].

However, the arms race that was [[History of the Forgotten War|The Great Forgotten War]] saw many [[Entities/Gods/Gods|Deities]] attempt to divert the ritual's channelling power to bolster their own. Until this point, [[Raven Queen|The First Elven Queen]] had been a neutral party in the war, however moments before the ritual completed, she used her new found divine fervour to attack all the gods attempting to steal her power. This outburst of negative energy cursed the [[Raven Queen]] and her followers into [[Shadowfell]], where she dissolved under her own outburst.

From this point on, history becomes slightly more clear. The [[Raven Queen]]'s new found divine power and continued followers, cursed to [[Shadowfell]], allowed her to transcend her need for a physical form. As such, she is speculated to be a combination of disconnected sensory perceptions, bound to the [[Shadowfell]], incapable of direct speech, but not reliant on mortal physicality nor singularity. As such, the [[Raven Queen]] is able to be at all places, in a place between death and life, in an afterlife-type state. Her fate no longer bound by anything but faith.

In the last moments of [[History of the Forgotten War|The Great Forgotten War]], the [[Raven Queen]] was targeted in her weakened ethereal state, erasing all memory of her existence from all elves. This almost entirely killed her, without the faith and memory of her followers. However, the [[Raven Queen]] was in the memory of many others; [[Shadowfell]] itself, [[Feywild]] creatures, her own mind...etc. She drew on these memories of her existence for years, until she was able to gain enough power enabling her to embed herself into the dreams of her followers once again... leading to the rise of today's [[Raven Queen]].

It is not known whether the [[Raven Queen]] we barely know today is the same kind and courageous [[Raven Queen|Queen of Elves]] that was known at the time. But, what is known, is that the [[Raven Queen]] materialised from the memories, twisted or not, of [[Raven Queen|The First Elven Queen]].

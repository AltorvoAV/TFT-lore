## Description:
[[Selûne|The Moonmaiden]] was the goddess of the Moon & Life. During multiple points of history, her portfolio expanded to navigation, beauty and purity, love and marriage, wanderers and seekers, diviners and dreamers, good and neutral lycanthropes, and autumn.

> "*Let all on whom my light falls be welcome if they desire to be so. As the silver moon waxes and wanes, so too does all life. Trust in my radiance, and know that all love alive under my light shall know my blessing. Turn to the moon, and I will be your true guide.*”
> 
> — [[Selûne]]

---
### Appearance / Avatar(s)
In both her avatars and her religious artwork, Selûne appeared in many forms, like the phases of the moon. 
1. One was a dusky-skinned human woman; perfect and exquisite beauty; wide, radiant, lime-green eyes; and long, ivory-hued hair that fell to her knees. 
2. Another was an ethereal young girl of slender frame, dark eyes, and hair, wearing diaphanous robes coloured white or resembling moonlight.
3. A third was a matronly middle-aged woman, with grey-streaked dark hair.
4. A simple depiction of the goddess was of a woman's face on the disc of the moon.

She was also ever changing, aging but ageless. If watched over time, her appearance seemed to grow to full radiance and fade away, in keeping with the waxing or waning of the moon.

In any form or state, whenever she was in darkness, Selûne's avatar continually emanated a faint blue-white glow, like moonlight.

## Personality
[[Selûne]]'s personality changed much like the phases of the moon. Most mortals and outsiders saw this as chaos, but her faithful saw this as her ability to guide and protect even whilst fluctuating between: enthusiastic, vivacious, joyous, majestic, subdued, motherly, poetic, tranquil, aggressive, fierce, cold, and merciless.

Such were her cycles. A constant reminder of change.

Generally, [[Selûne]] was caring, kind, accepting, and forgiving. Her ageless but ancient nature gave her a whimsical demeanour and wisdom above most all others. This led to her be slow to anger.

[[Selûne|The Moonmaiden]] was fiercely protective of all things good, especially when confronted with evil. She was generous, loving, and serenely peaceful.

## Worshipers
> "*Let your moon be my light, and I shall let my sword be your shining symbol.*”
> 
> — A basic prayer to [[Selûne]]

Members of the [[Church of Selûne]] or any that followed [[Selûne]] were called Selûnites.

People turned to [[Selûne]]'s warm embrace for protection, guidance, compassion, courage, or strength. She welcomed all those with good in their hearts, and protected those from evil.

You would find hundreds of worshipers for [[Selûne]] within almost every city on [[Nyropa]], as her love saw no bounds to the [[Material Plane]]. Many of these worshipers could be found out at sea, praying to [[Selûne|She Who Guides]] to see then home safely. One of the Selûnite's greatest place of worship was the [[Cathedral of Moonlight]]. Even those that would normally curse the moon (lycanthropes) were offered safe haven and protection in addition to guidance to hone their abilities and not let it consume them.

There was a time, where almost all marriages took place under moonlight to garner [[Selûne]]'s eternal love and protection.
### Tenets
The [[Church of Selûne]] gave teachings of self-reliance and finding one's own path. [[Selûne|The Moonmaiden]]'s strength and resolve made her a figure for female empowerment, that not only could she protect, but also guide with a mother's touch.

[[Selûne]]'s faithful were taught acceptance, tolerance, equality, and understanding; to treat all those other than one's self with love and kindness. To welcome all those who are lost or cold into [[Selûne|The Moonmaiden]]'s warmth, as she once gave to man's first.

[[Selûne]] was most revered for her acceptance of darkness, but never openly allowing it - to retaliate but not to let it devour. It intention was to shine light in all darkness, but "*not to rid the world of shade, for shade gives us clarity and further colours to paint the world with.*"

### Heresies
Those who believed in the [[Dark Moon Heresy]] held that Selûne and Shar were two faces of the same goddess.

## Abilities
Where Selûne's avatars walked, the night itself refused evil. Creatures of malice found themselves unwelcome, their forms unravelling beneath her gaze. She was immune to all light, lightning, illusion, enchantment, damage or charm affects cast against her.

She could freely banish any evil extraplanar being with one word, and cast any divination spells without cost.

To guide mortals who took her favour, [[Selûne]] would send owls, weredragons, or song dragons. Amongst these, owls were her favourite and became synonymous with [[Selûne|She Who Guides]].

---
## Relationships
[[Selûne]]'s greatest allies were her daughters. One of those being the first goddess of magic, who was born from essence of both [[Selûne]] & [[Shar]]. This made her the most powerful ally against [[Shar]].

All those who shared ideals with [[Selûne]] were counted as allies: [[Bahamut]], [[Raven Queen]], [[Ehlonna]], [[Moradin]], and [[Yondalla]] most of all.

Upon [[Selûne]]'s death, she entrusted her divine responsibility to [[Yondalla]], her oldest living daughter. [[Yondalla]] did not claim all of [[Selûne]]'s domains in her inheritance, perhaps out of hope for [[Selûne]]'s return.

Powerful mortals within the [[Feywild]] would also come to [[Selûne]]'s aid, most notably of these were: [[Titania, Queen of the Fairies]], [[Oberon, King of the Fairies]], and [[Raven Queen|The First Elven Queen]].

For enemies, she counted her twin sister [[Shar]] as her eternal foe. Their war could be felt in all [[Realms]] across the universe. They never forgot, nor forgave.

Her other great foes were [[Mask]], for his meddling and absence of her light within the shadows; [[Atropal]], for his desire to condemn all to undeath; later the [[Raven Queen]], for her meddling in and accidental corruption of Fey beings, though [[Selûne]] held no hatred against her; the [[Dead Three]], for their corruption of man.
